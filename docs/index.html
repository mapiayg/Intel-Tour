Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

The Intel-Tour ontology is designed to facilitate the planning and recommendation of tourist activities. It provides a structured framework for describing cities, attractions, events, hotels, restaurants, transportation and reviews. The ontology includes properties and relationships that enable the integration and querying of tourist-related data, supporting both human and machine-readable formats.

Introduction back to ToC

The Intel-Tour ontology defines a comprehensive model for representing tourist activities and related entities. It includes classes for cities, attractions, events, hotels, restaurants, transportation and reviews. This ontology aims to enhance the interoperability and accessibility of tourist information, making it easier to plan and recommend activities based on user preferences and reviews.

Rule 1back to ToC or SWRL ToC

If a hotel has 4 stars or more, then its type is "Luxury".

Hotel c(?h) , hotelStars dp(?h,?s) , greaterThan(?) -> hotelType dp(?h,"Luxury")

Rule 2back to ToC or SWRL ToC

If a review has a rating of 3 or more, then its comment is "Positive”.

Review c(?r) , rating dp(?r,?v) , greaterThan(?) -> comment dp(?r,"Positive")

Rule 3back to ToC or SWRL ToC

If the name of a restaurant is "PizzPass", then the name of its cousine is "Italian".

Restaurant c(?r) , restaurantName dp(?r,"PizzPass") , servesCousine op(?r,?c) -> cousineName dp(?c,"Italian")
